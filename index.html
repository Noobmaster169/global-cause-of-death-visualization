Non-Communicable Diseases

Majority of global deaths were caused by Non-Communicable Disease, mostly dominated by Cardiovascular Diseases and Neoplasms (Cancer). In developed countries, the rate is above 90%, whereas most less-developed countries have lower rates.

Infectious Diseases

Most countries have low rate of Infectious Disease case. However the number is very high among countries in Africa. Most commonly found causes are Lower Respiratory Infections, Diarrheal Diseases, Tuberculosis, HIV/AIDS, and Malaria, which are the biggest unsolved problems in the continent.

External Causes

Several cause of deaths comes from external factors. For example, 2% of death in United States and United Arab Emirates are caused by the use of drugs. Whereas, Latin America has high cases of death caused by Interpersonal Violence.
